Sentiment analysis in the Bible

Sep 15, 2017

I discovered sentiment analysis a few years ago, following interesting work by Matthew Jockers and Julia Silge. Sentiment analysis is a way to compare the emotional content of text by analyzing the frequency of certain words that reflect different emotions. This could be positivity or negativity or the use of specific emotional words.

One idea I wanted to do was analyze the sentiment of the Christian Bible. But before exploring this, I had a question: which version should I use?

That lead to a different question? How does sentiment vary in different Bible translations? Are some translations more positive than others?

Within the English-speaking world, there are many options for Bible translations. These options vary based on how they are translated, the source materials they use, and the language they use. Some translations are very literal and try to capture each word as literally as possible, whereas others are more dynamic, or a translation which tries to capture the thoughts behind the passages without being as literal.

In this comparison, we wanted to examine whether translations varied in their emotional content by using natural language processing techniques.

Emotionality

In order to assess emotionality, I added the average positive and negative sentment as calculated by the Stanford NLP method. This metric was done in order to test hypotheses about emotionality, since the positive and negative analyses seemed to suggest some translations may be more emotional overall than other translations. That means, some translations may have more positivity and negativity combined than other translations which may have less.

Valence

In addition to emotionality, we wanted to calculate the valence of each translation. This was done by subtracting negativity from positivity, to create a metric that showed the valence, positive or negative. This accounts for overall emotionality so that if a translation is high in positivity and high in negativity, it would be the same overall valence as a translation low in positivity and low in negativity.
